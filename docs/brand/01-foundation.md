# Ringdocket — Brand Foundation

**Status:** Draft v1 · April 22, 2026
**Owner:** Brian Mangum / LIGHTHOUSE 27 LLC

The load-bearing beliefs behind Ringdocket. Every downstream artifact — landing copy, App Store description, email tone, press pitches, PDF masthead — traces back here.

---

## 1. Purpose

**Ringdocket exists to make the spam economy legible.**

Every American with a phone has been trained into learned helplessness. The call comes in, you decline or report it, and nothing happens. Not publicly. Not ever. The scam economy runs on that silence — a $40 billion industry that depends on its victims not being able to see it, name it, or point at it.

The incumbent blockers sell that secrecy back as a feature. Their block list is a black box, their database is proprietary, your report is telemetry. None of them close the loop. *They profit from the same opacity the spammers do.* Meanwhile the information that would hold carriers and enforcement bodies accountable — FTC complaint feeds, FCC press releases, ITG tracebacks — is already public and consumer-invisible.

What changes because Ringdocket exists: a scam call stops being a lone, unrecorded event and becomes a cited line in a public ledger. The user gets the block. They also get the story. The campaign gets a name. The carrier gets attribution. When something happens downstream — an FCC fine, a traceback citation, a network-wide delisting — the person who first flagged the number finds out. *A phone call becomes a piece of evidence.*

## 2. Mission

We ship a shared block list to iPhones overnight, publish every report and every campaign as public record, and promote numbers to the block list only when three independent accounts corroborate within fourteen days.

When a fraud campaign hits the news, journalists cite the Ringdocket public record the way they cite court filings. Enforcement agencies reference our campaign pages in press releases. Carriers dread being named in the attribution chain. A hundred thousand paying subscribers carry Founding Flagger or first-flag credit on their profile. The quarterly Takedown Report is a civic document people actually read. "Ringdocket it" — *to put a scam on the record* — shows up in Reddit threads without us having to prompt it.

## 4. Core Values

Five values. Each is a decision filter. If a feature, copy line, or partnership violates one, we don't ship it.

### Transparency is the product.
Data lineage is first-class UI — source, corroboration, timestamp, attribution chain, all visible and citable.

- *We* publish the block list, campaign pages, and evidence ledger as public record.
- *We don't* run a proprietary database or hide provenance behind a "trust us" screen.

### Restraint is a signature.
Less color, less motion, fewer words, fewer badges. When something is loud, it has earned the volume.

- *We* use `#C2370A` as a seal, never a CTA, and lead home screens with narrative prose instead of stat grids.
- *We don't* ship confetti, leaderboards, streaks, exclamation points, emoji, or gradient hero numerals.

### Cite, don't claim.
Every assertion has a source. "Attributed takedown" requires a public citation. "Likely retired" is inference and we say so.

- *We* link FCC case numbers, ITG tracebacks, and FTC provenance on the same page as the claims that reference them.
- *We don't* round up, borrow credit, or use "99% of robocalls stopped" block-rate theater.

### Civic work earns receipts.
Users who report get credited. First-flag credit, corroboration attribution, Founding Flagger badge, itemized quarterly report.

- *We* give retroactive first-flag credit when a pending number promotes, and ship a personalized PDF quarterly naming the campaigns the user's reports helped retire.
- *We don't* gamify with daily streaks, busywork badges, or metrics a scammer's employee could farm.

### Private by default, public by choice.
On-device blocking. No call interception. No contact upload. No notes on public pages.

- *We* run the block via Apple's Call Directory Extension and publish only number + category + corroboration metadata.
- *We don't* upload address books, intercept audio, sell data, or render free-text notes on any public surface.

## 5. Positioning Statement

For Americans 30 to 65 tired of reporting spam numbers into a black box, Ringdocket is the public accountability ledger for the spam economy — an iOS block list plus a dashboard that names the campaign behind every call and cites who's being held to account. Unlike Truecaller, Hiya, RoboKiller, and Nomorobo, which compete on unverifiable block-rate claims and hide their data behind a wall, Ringdocket publishes every number, every report, and every attribution chain as public record — *and shows you when your report did something.*

---

## 6. Category Frame

Ringdocket is not a spam blocker. The category is saturated, incumbents own the keyword, and the user's mental model of "spam blocker" is pre-corrupted by a decade of vague promises.

Ringdocket is a **public accountability ledger for the spam economy.** The register is closer to a court docket or a newspaper of record than a utility app. The product is a block list on your phone the same way a newspaper is a stack of paper — the artifact is mundane; the function is civic.

Retention vocabulary: *ledger* (not database), *flag / corroborate / promote / retire* (not report / confirm / block / remove), *campaign* (not number), *case ID, first flag, attribution chain*, *Takedown Report*.

Acquisition headlines still say "silence spam calls" — reactance in a 30–65 audience kills civic-led copy. Once the user is through the door, the frame shifts. *They arrive for the quiet. They stay for the docket.*

## 8. Origin Narrative

> Specifics of Brian's personal inflection point are **inferred** — not documented. Structural claims (LIGHTHOUSE 27 LLC, portfolio of civic-utility products, FTC-feed realization, public-data thesis) are documented. Anything about a single triggering call should be replaced with Brian's own account before public use.

Ringdocket began with a realization, not a call. Brian Mangum — operating as LIGHTHOUSE 27 LLC out of North Carolina, building small civic-utility products like SCDMV Alerts and Pottydirectory — opened the FTC's Do Not Call complaint feed in early 2026 and found a river of data nobody was piping to consumers. Millions of complaints. Named scam categories. Geographic clusters. Carrier names. All public. None of it surfaced in any of the six spam-blocker apps on his phone.

*(Inferred:)* Around the same time, someone close to him nearly handed a Medicare number to a caller claiming to be from CMS. The gap between what was knowable about that call — the campaign, the carrier, the open FCC traceback — and what the person on the line saw — an unknown number, a confident voice, a fabricated deadline — was the product.

The thesis has not moved since. Spam calls are a surface problem, not a detection problem. The data already exists; the missing artifact is a ledger. Ringdocket is that ledger, packaged as the one category where iOS users reliably pay a subscription — a call blocker — and designed so the civic identity is something users grow into, never something we recruit them into. *Quiet the phone first. Show the ledger when they're ready to look.*
